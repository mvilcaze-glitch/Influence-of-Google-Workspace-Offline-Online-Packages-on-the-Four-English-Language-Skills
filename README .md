1. Presentation

This program implements a pedagogical intervention at Antigravity IDE, Arequipa, to test the influence of Google Workspace offline-online packages on the four English skills. 

Problem Context at Antigravity IDE:  
Students at Antigravity IDE face intermittent internet connectivity in classrooms. Computer labs have stable connection, but mobile data is limited. Google Workspace for Education is active, but offline mode is disabled by default. 

Research Hypotheses:  
HE1: The technical offline configuration and data persistence of Google Workspace significantly improve receptive skills of listening and reading comprehension in English among Cycle VI and Cycle VII students at Antigravity IDE.  
HE2: The management of asynchronous synchronized tasks significantly optimizes the technical and communicative quality of oral and written production in English in the evaluated institutional environment at Antigravity IDE.

Target Population:  
Students from Cycle VI and Cycle VII at Antigravity IDE, aged 12-16.  
Timeline: March to December 2026, 32 weeks, 2 hours per week.  
Implementation Site: Classrooms 201-204 and Computer Lab 1, Antigravity IDE, Arequipa.

---

2. Implementation at Antigravity IDE

2.1 Technical Setup  
The IT department at Antigravity IDE will enable offline mode in Google Workspace Admin Console one week before the intervention. Google Drive offline folders will be configured on institutional Chromebooks and student devices. All materials, audio files, and Google Forms will be pre-downloaded. A sync test will be conducted with a sample group of students to verify data persistence before starting.

2.2 Assessment Instruments per Session  
Each 90-minute lesson includes four instruments that function offline and sync for instant feedback:

- Self-Evaluation Checklist: Google Forms offline with “I can” statements for listening, speaking, reading, and writing. Students rate themselves 1-4. Results sync and display an instant level: A = Excellent, B = Good, C = Needs Practice.
- Instant Feedback Quiz: Google Forms offline with 5 items per skill. 
- Reflection Prompt: One question in Google Docs offline: “What did I learn today? What can I improve? What would I do differently?”
- Motivational Message: Each session begins with a short quote on effort and growth. 

All evidence is stored in individual Google Drive portfolios managed by Antigravity IDE.

2.3 Data Collection for HE1 and HE2  
- HE1: Quiz scores and completion rates from Google Forms offline measure listening and reading comprehension.
- HE2: Speaking recordings, writing drafts, and version history in Google Docs measure technical and communicative quality of production. Google Sheets tracks submission timestamps to analyze asynchronous task management.

2.4 Resources at Antigravity IDE  
- Institutional Chromebooks available for Cycle VI and VII  
- 1 IT technician for setup and troubleshooting  
- Google Workspace for Education account under http://antigravity.edu.pe domain  

2.5 Roles and Ethics  
Teacher Role: Facilitator and asynchronous feedback provider through Google Docs comments after synchronization.  
Student Role: Work in offline mode during class, sync materials at home or in lab time, complete self-evaluation per session, maintain Drive portfolio.  
Ethics: Parental consent obtained through Antigravity IDE protocols. Data anonymized for thesis analysis.

3. Cycle VI - [Grade 1, 2, 3 Secondary]  
Level: A2 | Lessons: 1-32 | Institution: Antigravity IDE

Lesson 1: Hello and Verb to be  
Motivational Message: “Every expert was once a beginner.”  
HE1 Focus: Listen to greetings offline. Read a short profile offline. Complete quiz in Google Forms offline.  
HE2 Focus: Write 5 sentences in Google Docs offline. Record a 30-sec self-introduction and upload to Drive.  
Assessment: Quiz, self-evaluation checklist, reflection prompt, attendance in Google Sheets.

Lesson 16: Unit 3 Review  
Motivational Message: “Progress is better than perfection.”  
HE1 Focus: Listening and reading review quiz offline.  
HE2 Focus: Group project in Google Slides offline. Record a 1-min presentation.  

Lesson 32: Cycle VI Final Assessment  
Motivational Message: “You did not come this far to only come this far.”  
Assessment: Final listening and reading test in Forms offline, recorded speaking presentation, final writing task in Docs offline, full cycle self-evaluation. Final grade: A, B, C based on portfolio average and improvement evidence.

Lessons 2-31 follow the same structure.

---

4. Cycle VII - [Grade 4, 5 Secondary]  
Level: A2-B1 | Lessons: 33-64 | Institution: Antigravity IDE

Lesson 33: Present Perfect Introduction  
Motivational Message: “Small steps every day lead to big results.”  
HE1 Focus: Listen to interviews offline. Reading quiz in Forms offline.  
HE2 Focus: Write a paragraph offline. Record a 45-sec audio sharing experiences.  

Lesson 48: Preparing for a debate: School uniforms  
Motivational Message: “Your voice matters. Speak with purpose.”  
HE1 Focus: Listen to model debates offline. Reading comprehension quiz offline.  
HE2 Focus: Draft arguments offline, revise based on async comments, record argument delivery.  

Lesson 64: Cycle VII Final Assessment  
Motivational Message: “The future depends on what you do today.”  
Assessment: Final listening and reading test in Forms offline, final oral presentation recorded and graded, final essay in Docs offline, full cycle self-evaluation and action plan. Final grade: A, B, C based on portfolio, improvement rate, and reflection quality.

Lessons 34-63 follow the same structure, ensuring every session produces data for HE1 and HE2.

5. Expected Outcomes for Antigravity IDE

1. Measurable improvement in listening and reading scores due to offline access and data persistence.
2. Higher quality in speaking and writing due to asynchronous feedback cycles and revision.
3. Student portfolio in Google Drive showing progression from C to A level across 64 lessons.
4. Replicable model for other institutions in Arequipa with similar connectivity issues.
